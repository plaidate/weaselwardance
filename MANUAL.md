# Weasel War Dance — Player's Manual

*A rhythm romp through the mustelid family: hit the beat, hypnotize your dinner.*

## The story

Stoats and weasels really do this: they twist, hop and flip in a frenzied
"war dance" that seems to leave their prey too baffled to run. In Weasel
War Dance the trick finally gets the soundtrack it deserves. Work your way
up the family tree, from a least weasel twitching at a vole to a giant
otter raving at a caiman — eight dancers, eight songs, one entranced
audience of one.

## Controls

| Input | Action |
|---|---|
| **D-pad left / right** | hop left / hop right |
| **D-pad up** | jump |
| **D-pad down** | crouch |
| **A** | pounce |
| **B** | dook (the happy weasel chirp) |
| **Crank** | spin — keep it turning for the length of the coil |

On the dancer select screen: crank (or d-pad left/right) to browse,
**A** to dance, **B** back to the title. The System Menu has a
**reset save** item if you want a fresh family.

## How to play

Cues stream from the right along the bottom track toward the **hit ring**
on the left. Answer each cue with its button exactly as it reaches the
ring:

- **Arrows** (left/right/up/down) — hop with the matching d-pad direction
- **Paw print** — pounce with **A**
- **Chirp arcs** — dook with **B**
- **A coil of circles** — a crank spin: crank continuously until the coil
  runs out. The circles fill in as your rotation builds; you need about
  half a turn per beat (180°/sec) to finish it "DIZZYING". Slower cranking
  can still salvage a "WOBBLY".

Every song starts with a one-bar count-in of clicks (and a big countdown
number) so you can find the beat. The music is a step sequencer driven by
the same clock as the judgment, so what you hear is exactly what you're
judged against — it cannot drift.

Timing grades: **PERFECT** is a tight window (±0.06s), **GOOD** is looser
(±0.13s). Pressing a button when no matching cue is near the ring is a
**FLUB** — it breaks your combo and unsettles the prey, so don't mash.
Letting a cue sail past unanswered is a **MISS**, which hurts even more.

### The hypno meter

The HYPNO bar (top right) shows how entranced your prey is right now.
PERFECTs and finished spins push it up; misses and flubs knock it down.
Watch the prey stage right: past a certain point its eyes start to
spiral, it sways on the spot, and at very high hypnosis little hearts
appear. Stumble and it flinches right out of the trance.

## Scoring, ranks and progression

- PERFECT = 100 points, GOOD = 50 (half!)
- Full crank spin ("DIZZYING") = 150, partial ("WOBBLY") = 75
- Your result is a percentage of the song's maximum (all PERFECTs, all
  spins finished)
- **Catch the prey at 60% or better** — that unlocks the next, larger
  mustelid
- Ranks: **S** 95+, **A** 85+, **B** 72+, **C** 60+, **D** below
- Combos count consecutive hits; every 25 in a row earns confetti and a
  jingle. Misses and flubs reset it.
- Best percentage and rank per song are saved on the device

## Cues and reactions at a glance

- **Hop / jump / crouch cues** — the bread and butter; the mink's chart
  slides them off the beat, the pine marten ladders up-down-up-down
- **Pounce (A)** — the honey badger's whole vocabulary; big lunging move
- **Dook (B)** — comes in quick pairs in the ferret's disco
- **Crank spins** — first taught by the stoat; the giant otter saves an
  extra-long one for the end
- **The prey** — startles (and hops in fright) whenever you miss;
  mesmerize it to 60%+ by the final note to catch it

## Tips

1. **PERFECT is worth double.** The music and the judgment share one
   clock — trust your ears as much as the track. Locking to the beat pays
   far better than reacting to the glyphs.
2. **Never mash.** A press with no cue in range is a flub: combo gone,
   hypnosis dented. Rests are part of the dance.
3. **Crank spins are the biggest single prizes** (150 points). Crank
   faster than feels necessary — you can't over-crank, but falling short
   of about 55% of the needed rotation scores nothing at all.
4. **A miss hurts the trance three times more than a flub.** If you're
   fumbling for the right button, a late GOOD inside the ±0.13s window
   still beats letting the cue pass.
5. **60% clears.** All-GOODs alone only gets you 50%, so you need some
   PERFECTs or finished spins to catch the prey — aim your focus at the
   spin coils first.
6. **Combo is for glory, not points.** Your percentage decides the catch
   and the rank; the max combo on the results screen is between you and
   the wolverine.
